# Product

## Register

`product`

Medário is a product-first local health discovery platform with a brand-led regional identity. Use **product mode** for search, profiles, onboarding, doctor dashboards, analytics, SEO/Ads tools, and administrative workflows. Use **brand mode** only for the public landing page, launch campaigns, press material, and investor/business-plan pages.

## Product Name

**Medário**

A medical directory and visibility platform born in Joinville/SC. The name suggests a curated medical index: a place where people find doctors and doctors become easier to find.

## Product One-Liner

**Medário connects people in Joinville to trustworthy medical care — and helps doctors become findable with clarity, ethics, and local intelligence.**

## Users

### 1. Patients and families

People in Joinville and nearby cities who need to find a physician by specialty, location, availability, insurance, private appointment, language, accessibility, telemedicine option, or clinical focus. They are often anxious, time-constrained, and tired of messy searches across Google, WhatsApp, Instagram, and outdated clinic pages.

### 2. Doctors

Physicians who want a professional, credible, locally optimized digital presence without becoming influencers. They need profile visibility, structured information, SEO support, Google Business Profile optimization, Ads guidance, analytics, and a defensible way to be found by the right patients.

### 3. Clinics and secretaries

Operational users who need to keep profiles, addresses, phone numbers, specialties, appointment links, schedules, insurance lists, and photos updated without technical friction.

### 4. Internal Medário team

The team that validates profiles, audits content quality, handles onboarding, monitors search quality, runs SEO/Ads experiments, and protects the trust layer of the platform.

## Product Purpose

Medário exists to make local medical search less random.

Success means:

1. A patient can find a relevant physician faster than they would through generic Google search or fragmented social media.
2. A doctor can present a credible professional profile without depending on generic marketplace aesthetics, paid ranking tricks, or social-media performance.
3. The platform creates a trustworthy local data layer for healthcare discovery in Joinville, then expands carefully across Santa Catarina.

## Core Jobs To Be Done

### For patients

- Find doctors by specialty, subspecialty, location, insurance, private care, telemedicine, accessibility, language, and clinical focus.
- Compare profiles without feeling manipulated by opaque rankings.
- Understand what each doctor treats, where they work, how to schedule, and what information is verified.
- Save, share, and contact a doctor with minimal friction.

### For doctors

- Claim or create a professional profile.
- Improve local discoverability on Medário and external search engines.
- Receive structured recommendations for profile completeness, SEO, Google Business Profile, Ads, and analytics.
- Track profile views, contact actions, search appearances, and conversion signals.
- Maintain ethical, medically appropriate positioning.

### For the platform

- Build a high-quality local directory before scaling geographically.
- Prioritize verified, structured, useful information over inflated marketing copy.
- Separate organic relevance, verification, sponsorship, and profile completeness visibly.
- Avoid medical claims that cannot be substantiated.

## Positioning

Medário is not a national appointment marketplace trying to own every transaction. It is a **regional trust-and-discovery layer** for medical care.

The first strategic wedge is **Joinville/SC**: local identity, local search behavior, local clinics, local streets, local specialties, local SEO. Expansion to Santa Catarina should only happen after the Joinville data layer, profile quality, and acquisition engine are strong.

## Product Personality

**Clinical, local, refined, useful.**

Medário should feel like a competent medical concierge and a serious local institution — not a startup screaming for attention.

Three-word personality:

**trustworthy, regional, precise**

## Voice

### Use

- Direct, calm, practical language.
- Short explanations before action.
- Patient-facing copy that reduces anxiety.
- Doctor-facing copy that respects professional seriousness.
- Transparent ranking and sponsorship language.

### Avoid

- Hype: “the best doctors”, “revolutionary”, “game-changing”.
- Fear: “don’t risk your health”, “urgent”, “dangerous if you don’t act”.
- Fake warmth: “we care so much about your journey”.
- Generic SaaS verbs: boost, supercharge, unlock, skyrocket.
- Medical promises: cure, guaranteed result, superior doctor, fastest treatment.

## Anti-References

Medário must be the opposite of:

- Generic medical directories with stock photos, blue gradients, heart icons, and thin trust claims.
- Marketplace patterns that make physicians look like commodities.
- Social-media doctor branding that rewards performance over clarity.
- Municipality/government visual language: no brasão, no prefeitura look, no official-symbol mimicry.
- Hospital-blue interfaces with cold, bureaucratic density.
- AI-generated SaaS clichés: purple-to-blue gradients, glassmorphism, nested cards, fake 3D icons, rounded-square icon tiles everywhere.
- Ranking systems that imply “best doctor” without transparent criteria.

## Design Principles

1. **Local before generic.** Every major surface should carry subtle Joinville DNA: Alameda/Rua das Palmeiras geometry, architectural arches, Cidade das Flores cues, map/grid references, or refined regional language.

2. **Trust through structure.** Verification, specialty taxonomy, profile completeness, source of information, update date, and sponsorship status must be clear.

3. **Useful beats decorative.** If an element does not help orientation, trust, comparison, or action, remove it.

4. **Premium without luxury cosplay.** The brand may feel refined, but never exclusive, elitist, or inaccessible.

5. **Medical without clichés.** No stethoscope logo, no generic heart, no oversized red cross. The medical cue is secondary and controlled.

6. **Search is the product.** The search experience, filters, profile cards, and doctor pages are the core proof. Landing pages serve discovery, not vanity.

7. **Ethics are visible.** Sponsored results, featured profiles, verification, and organic relevance must be labeled in plain language.

8. **Designed for anxious users.** Patients often search under stress. Prioritize legibility, reassurance, and fast decisions.

## Trust Model

Medário should distinguish:

- **Verified profile**: identity and professional registration checked.
- **Updated profile**: information recently reviewed.
- **Claimed profile**: managed by doctor/clinic or authorized representative.
- **Sponsored placement**: paid visibility, clearly labeled.
- **Organic relevance**: ranking based on specialty, location, availability, completeness, and user query match.

Do not visually blur these categories.

## Ranking Rules

Search ranking should never imply medical quality unless the underlying criterion is explicit and defensible.

Allowed ranking signals:

- Specialty/query match.
- Location proximity.
- Profile completeness.
- Verification status.
- Availability or scheduling clarity.
- Insurance/private-care match.
- Recent profile update.
- Sponsored status, when clearly labeled.

Disallowed ranking language:

- “Best doctors”.
- “Top physicians” without a transparent methodology.
- “Most trusted” unless based on a defined, published trust metric.
- “Recommended by Medário” unless criteria are explicit.

## Growth Strategy

Start narrow.

Phase 1: Joinville core specialties and high-intent searches.

Phase 2: Joinville clinic and doctor acquisition through profile completeness, SEO, and Google Business Profile optimization.

Phase 3: Specialty landing pages and local content engine.

Phase 4: Expansion to nearby cities in northern Santa Catarina.

Phase 5: Statewide Santa Catarina taxonomy and acquisition engine.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Baseline: WCAG AA on all public and product surfaces.

Commitments:

- High contrast for all body text, filters, buttons, badges, and map labels.
- Keyboard navigation for search, filters, profile cards, menus, and dashboards.
- Visible focus states.
- Responsive layouts for mobile-first medical search.
- Reduced-motion support.
- Plain-language labels for patients.
- No color-only signaling for verification, sponsored status, errors, or availability.

## AI / Agent Rules

When an AI agent works on this repository:

- Read this file before designing or changing product flows.
- Read `DESIGN.md` before creating components, pages, tokens, or visual variants.
- Do not invent a new visual system for each page.
- Do not add medical ranking claims without explicit product rules.
- Do not use generic health icons unless approved in the design system.
- Do not create page-specific components when a reusable primitive should exist.
- Treat profile trust, search clarity, and ethical labeling as product requirements, not polish.
